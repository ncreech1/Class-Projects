Here is a summary of the problem:

  • You are given two vectors of integers, X and Y.
  • The two vectors are the same size and contain integers between -1000 and 1000.
  • The vectors define cartesian coordinates. The points are (X[i],Y[i]) for 0 ≤ i < X.size().
  • Suppose you draw a rectangle whose sides are parallel to the x and y axes, and suppose that the rectangle contains all of the points.
  • Return the smallest possible area of a rectangle that does this.
  • The problem will guarantee that the area will be greater than zero.

Try this one on your own first. I suggest graphing examples 1 and 2, and the answer should fall out pretty naturally.

The bounding box will be defined by the:

  • Minimum x value, minx
  • Maximum x value, maxx
  • Minimum y value, miny
  • Maximum y value, maxy

The width of the bounding box is maxx-minx, and the height is maxy-miny.
